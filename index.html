An autonomous institution established jointly by the Science & Technology Department, Govt. of Kerala and the Department of Atomic Energy, Govt. of India.

About Us

Kerala School of Mathematics (KSoM) is an advanced research institute in Mathematics which started in 2007 as a joint venture of the Department of Atomic Energy (DAE), Government of India and the Science and Technology Department, Government of Kerala. KSoM was founded with the vision of establishing a centre of excellence for research in Mathematics and to revive the glory of a school by a similar name which flourished between the fourteenth and sixteenth century in Kerala.

The main objective of Kerala School of Mathematics is conduct of high quality Mathematical Research. Our mission is to create a vibrant academic environment that promotes cutting-edge research, nurtures young mathematicians, and contributes significantly to the global mathematical community.

Research Areas at KSoM

1. Complex Analysis

Investigation of the impact of Hörmander's estimates on various properties of the Bers domain and exploring the profound implications of the remarkable fact that the Kobayashi metric is isometric to the Teichmüller metric. Research sits at the intersection of complex analysis, Teichmüller theory, and differential geometry.

Read More →

2. Braid Groups & Mapping Class Groups

Investigation of intricate relationships between braid groups and mapping class groups, with particular emphasis on liftability problems and the Birman-Hilden property. Central focus on unitary representations of braid groups, their classification, irreducibility properties, and connections to quantum invariants.

Read More →

3. Non-commutative Functional Analysis

Investigation of quantum aspects of approximation and convexity within the framework of operator spaces and operator systems in C*-algebras. Research includes completely bounded approximation property, quantum convexity, matricial ranges, and structure theory of operator systems.

Read More →

4. Harmonic Analysis

Research on modulation spaces and their invariance properties, Fourier multipliers, twisted convolution operators, maximal functions along hypersurfaces, Hardy-Sobolev inequalities in non-commutative settings, and spherical maximal operators on symmetric spaces.

Read More →

5. Analytic Number Theory

Research on multiple zeta values and their generalizations, explicit averages of multiplicative functions, Möbius sums with coprimality conditions, and chromatic generalizations of classical results.

Read More →

Our Programmes

Integrated M.Sc. - Ph.D. Programme

The program is an advanced postgraduate training program designed to prepare students for a research career in Mathematics, with options to specialize in Physics or Theoretical Computer Science. Key features include a monthly scholarship of Rs. 6000 for MSc students and a scholarship equivalent to national schemes for PhD candidates. The program offers subsidized on-campus accommodation and free access to institute facilities. Eligibility requires a B.Sc., B.Math., B.Stat., or equivalent degree with Mathematics as a subject, or a BE/B.Tech. degree.

Learn More →

Ph.D. in Mathematics

The PhD programme at Kerala School of Mathematics provides opportunities to explore various research areas in depth. Students are selected through examinations followed by an interview at Kerala School of Mathematics. To be eligible, candidates must have a Master's degree in the Mathematical or Physical Sciences, or a B.E./B.Tech. (or equivalent degree) in an appropriate field of Engineering or Technology. Additionally, candidates should have passed one of the specified entrance tests. The PhD offer will be based on the candidate's performance in the interview. All students admitted to the PhD program will have to complete 24 credits in their first year.

Learn More →

Postdoctoral Fellowships

The Postdoctoral Fellowship program welcomes holders of INSPIRE faculty, NBHM postdoctoral fellowship, N-PDF, or any other external postdoctoral fellowship. The Research Associateship is offered to young researchers who have completed or submitted their PhD dissertation in Mathematics or related areas. Research Associates are expected to teach at least one course each year at the MSc level. The fellowship amount varies based on experience, with additional contingency grants provided. The fellowship is initially for one year, extendable based on a review of the work done.
